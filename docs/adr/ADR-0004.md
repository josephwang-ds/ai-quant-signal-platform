# ADR-0004: Use Events and AI as Governed Workflow Participants

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-07-13
- **Decision owners:** Architecture maintainers
- **Scope:** Workflow integration and agent authority

## Context

Research lifecycles involve long-running validation, review, monitoring, scheduling, and AI-assisted interpretation. Direct synchronous coupling would make modules brittle. At the same time, agents and background jobs must not bypass business rules or become untraceable sources of truth.

## Decision

Use past-tense domain events to announce committed business facts and trigger decoupled Application workflows. Event handlers are idempotent and observable. Begin with in-process delivery and transactional reliability appropriate to the modular monolith; preserve contracts that can later support durable delivery.

Schedulers and AI agents are external actors. They consume events and read through approved ports, but every mutation goes through an Application use case. AI may interpret, summarize, compare, and draft; deterministic domain policies retain authority over validation, risk, and lifecycle transitions.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Workflows remain decoupled from adapters and agent providers.
- Agent activity is auditable and subject to the same invariants as human activity.
- Event contracts provide a path to asynchronous scaling.

### Trade-offs

- Idempotency, correlation, retry, and failure handling are mandatory concerns.
- Some read models may be eventually consistent.
- AI output requires provenance and review metadata.

## Alternatives considered

- **Direct module-to-module calls for all workflows:** rejected because coupling grows with lifecycle complexity.
- **Agents mutate repositories directly:** rejected because it bypasses invariants and accountability.
- **LLM-based validation or risk authority:** rejected because those decisions must be deterministic and reproducible.

## Compliance

- Events describe facts and carry stable identifiers and correlation metadata.
- Consumers tolerate duplicate delivery.
- Agents and schedulers use Application ports only.
- Durable AI artifacts distinguish evidence, calculation, assumption, and interpretation.
