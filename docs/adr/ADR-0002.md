# ADR-0002: Organize the Domain Around Bounded Contexts and Aggregates

- **Status:** Accepted
- **Date:** 2026-07-13
- **Decision owners:** Architecture maintainers
- **Scope:** Domain model and ownership

## Context

The workspace is Strategy-centric but spans research, validation, governance, portfolio, and market intelligence. A page-centric or shared-record model would blur ownership, couple workflows, and make lifecycle rules difficult to protect.

## Decision

Use Domain-Driven Design with the frozen bounded contexts: Research, Validation, Governance, Portfolio, and Market Intelligence. Use aggregate roots as consistency boundaries, including Research, ValidationRun, Evaluation, Portfolio, and MarketDataset as defined by the Architecture Bible.

Strategy is the central lifecycle identity. Contexts contribute their owned records to its governed history without sharing mutable internal models. Aggregates reference external aggregates by identity and enforce their own invariants.

## Consequences

### Positive

- Business vocabulary and ownership become explicit.
- State transitions and invariants have authoritative homes.
- Modules can evolve without a universal data model.

### Trade-offs

- Some concepts require translation between contexts.
- Cross-aggregate workflows need Application coordination and eventual consistency where appropriate.
- Duplicate read representations may be legitimate.

## Alternatives considered

- **Page- or feature-centric domain:** rejected because UI navigation is not business ownership.
- **Single shared Strategy object graph:** rejected because it creates an oversized consistency boundary.
- **Database-schema-first design:** rejected because persistence would dictate the business model.

## Compliance

- New domain terms identify an owning context.
- Aggregate mutations occur through behavior, not generic status updates.
- Cross-context models are translated at boundaries.
