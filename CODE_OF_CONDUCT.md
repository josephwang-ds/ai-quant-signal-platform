# Code of Conduct

## Our commitment

We are committed to making participation in AI Quant Research Workspace respectful, safe, and productive for everyone, regardless of experience, identity, background, nationality, language, disability, appearance, or personal characteristics.

This project values rigorous disagreement. Critique ideas, evidence, architecture, and code directly; treat the people contributing them with patience and dignity.

## Expected behavior

Community members are expected to:

- communicate with empathy, precision, and respect;
- welcome questions and help others build context;
- give actionable feedback focused on observable behavior and project standards;
- acknowledge uncertainty, correct mistakes, and credit contributions;
- respect privacy, confidentiality, and responsible-disclosure boundaries;
- keep research and financial claims appropriately qualified; and
- prioritize community safety and project integrity over winning an argument.

## Unacceptable behavior

The following behavior is not acceptable:

- harassment, intimidation, discrimination, threats, or personal attacks;
- sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted attention;
- trolling, sustained disruption, or deliberately inflammatory communication;
- publishing private information, credentials, research data, or correspondence without permission;
- impersonation, plagiarism, or knowingly misleading technical or research claims;
- pressuring contributors to disclose personal information or private security details; and
- retaliation against anyone who raises a good-faith concern.

## Scope

This Code applies in repository issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, commits, project events, and other spaces where someone represents the project. It also applies to private interactions that materially affect community safety or participation.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior:

1. Do not disclose sensitive details in a public issue.
2. Contact the repository maintainer, [@josephwang-ds](https://github.com/josephwang-ds), through a private contact method listed on the maintainer’s GitHub profile.
3. If no private method is available, open a minimal public issue asking for confidential contact without describing the incident.

Reports should include the relevant location, approximate time, people involved, what occurred, and any supporting material you can safely share. Reports will be handled as confidentially as practical. Information will be shared only with people needed to assess and address the concern.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may edit or remove contributions and may issue a clarification, warning, temporary restriction, or permanent ban when behavior violates this Code. The response will consider impact, intent, history, safety, and willingness to repair harm.

Maintainers who have a conflict of interest must step away from handling the report when another qualified decision-maker is available. Good-faith reports and appeals will not result in retaliation.

## Appeals

A person subject to enforcement may request reconsideration by contacting the maintainer privately and explaining relevant context or corrective action. Appeals do not pause urgent safety measures. The resulting decision and rationale will be documented privately.

## Project standard

This policy is intentionally specific to this repository and is informed by widely adopted open-source community standards. It may evolve through normal review as the maintainer community grows.
